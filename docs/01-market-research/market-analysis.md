## 6. PORTER'S FIVE FORCES

### 1. Competitive Rivalry — LOW
No luxury concierge competitor in MK. High-street cleaners compete on price, not premium positioning. National apps lack local relationships. Rating: **2/5 threat**

### 2. Threat of New Entrants — MEDIUM
Low barriers to entry (no equipment needed). Brand, relationships, and operations moat take time to build. Rating: **3/5 threat**

### 3. Supplier Power — LOW-MEDIUM
Multiple dry cleaners available in MK. We spread across 3-4 partners. No single supplier can hold us hostage. Rating: **2.5/5 threat**

### 4. Buyer Power — MEDIUM
Affluent customers have options. Loyalty through memberships, quality, and relationships reduces churn. Rating: **3/5 threat**

### 5. Threat of Substitutes — LOW
Customers could: (a) do laundry themselves — but won't at this income level; (b) use high-street dry cleaners — inconvenient; (c) use apps — but not luxury. Rating: **2/5 threat**

**Overall Porter's Score: FAVOURABLE — market is highly attractive.**

## 9. BLUE OCEAN STRATEGY

### Four Actions Framework

**ELIMINATE** (from traditional dry cleaners):
- High-street shops (no shop needed)
- Drop-off/collection inconvenience
- Unclear pricing
- Impersonal service
- Generic bags/hangers

**REDUCE:**
- Customer effort to zero
- Turnaround time (below 48 hours standard)
- Uncertainty (live tracking)
- Payment friction (auto-billing)

**RAISE:**
- Service standards (luxury white-glove)
- Communication quality (personal account manager)
- Packaging (branded premium bags)
- Transparency (garment tracking, photo evidence)
- Trust (insurance, guarantees)

**CREATE (New Value):**
- Garment concierge model (nobody else in MK has this)
- Membership/subscription plans
- Dedicated corporate accounts
- Wedding dress preservation specialist
- Designer/luxury item specialist
- Garment care consultation service

### Blue Ocean Canvas
The Garment Concierge is not competing with dry cleaners. It is creating a new category: **The Luxury Garment Management Company.**
