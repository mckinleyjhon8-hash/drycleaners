## AVATAR 1: THE LONDON COMMUTER EXECUTIVE

**Name:** James Harrington
**Age:** 42
**Location:** Broughton, Milton Keynes (MK10)
**Occupation:** VP of Operations, Financial Services (Canary Wharf)
**Income:** £145,000/year

### Demographics
- Married, 2 children (ages 9 and 12)
- House value: £650,000
- Drives: BMW 5 Series
- Commutes: MK Central → London Euston, 5 days/week

### Psychographics
- Values: Efficiency, status, quality, reliability
- Lifestyle: Gym at 6am, London by 8am, back by 7:30pm
- Self-image: Successful professional who has earned the right to premium services
- Mindset: "If it saves me an hour a week, it's worth £50/month"

### Wardrobe
- 8 tailored suits (avg value £1,200 each)
- 20+ formal shirts
- 3 overcoats
- Regular need: Weekly shirt service + monthly suit care

### Pain Points
- Dry cleaner is closed when he leaves (7am) and when he returns (8pm)
- Has to ask his wife to drop items off — causes tension
- Has ruined a £1,400 suit at a discount cleaner before
- Doesn't trust local cleaners with his Tom Ford or Brioni pieces

### Buying Triggers
- "I can book it on my phone Sunday night, it's collected Monday morning"
- "They handle designer garments — they know what they're doing"
- "I have a dedicated account, they know my preferences"
- Tried a membership for Amazon Prime mentality — wants same for garment care

### Objections
- "Is it really better than my current cleaner?"
- "What if they lose something?"
- "I don't want another subscription"

### Messaging That Works
*"Your garments collected before 8am, returned by 6pm. While you're in London, we're caring for your wardrobe."*

### LTV Estimate
- Monthly spend: £200-350
- Retention: 36+ months
- LTV: **£7,200-£12,600**
