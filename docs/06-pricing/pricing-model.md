# Pricing Model — The Garment Concierge
## Per-order concierge pricing + optional membership

**Last updated:** 2026-06-14
**Status:** Working model. Trade rates are assumptions pending negotiation (see §9).

---

## 1. Philosophy

1. **Pay-per-item is the core.** Dry-cleaning demand is lumpy and irregular, so
   per-order pricing self-matches demand and proves a margin on *every* order.
2. **Membership is a loyalty layer, not bundled cleaning.** A low monthly fee buys
   perks (free delivery, members' pricing, priority) — it is near-pure margin and
   drives frequency. We do **not** sell all-you-can-eat bundles (usage risk + churn).
3. **We compete on experience + aggregation, not price.** Fluff & Tumble already
   offers cheap direct collection/delivery (free over £15). We win the affluent,
   time-poor segment with a premium experience and by routing each item to the best
   specialist cleaner — something a customer can't easily do themselves.

## 3. Sourcing strategy — two anchor partners

Your competitor comparison shows no single cleaner wins everything. Route by category:

| Category | Best partner | Why (from comparison data) |
|---|---|---|
| Suits, shirts, outerwear, bedding, everyday | **Fluff & Tumble** | Cheapest across most standard items; free delivery £15; 24–48h |
| Leather, suede, bridal, UGG, handbags, specialist | **One Stop** | Cheapest on leather/suede/wedding/UGG/designer items |

**Target trade rate:** 25–35% off the cleaner's retail for committed weekly volume
(we bring demand + handle marketing/logistics). Model below uses a conservative **30%**.

## 5. Membership — "The Concierge Membership"

**£19.99 / month.** Benefits:
- Free collection & delivery on every order (no minimum)
- 15% off all cleaning
- Priority scheduling + express slots
- A dedicated garment-care concierge

**Why it works:**
- The £19.99 fee is **near-pure margin** (we're not pre-committing cleaning).
- The 15% discount is funded by the 50%+ markup — a member order still nets ~40%+.
- It removes the "am I using it enough?" anxiety that kills bundle subscriptions.
- Even at **one order/month** a member is profitable, and members order *more*
  because collection is friction-free.

**Why not all-you-can-eat bundles:** heavy users cost more than the fee (we lose
money); light users churn. Worst fit for lumpy demand.

## 6. Per-order unit economics

**Typical pay-as-you-go basket:** 1 suit + 3 shirts + 1 dress.

| Line | Amount |
|---|---|
| Customer pays (concierge) | £24 + 3×£4.50 + £20 = **£57.50** |
| We pay cleaner (trade) | £10.47 + 3×£2.07 + £8.40 = **£25.08** |
| **Gross spread** | **£32.42 (56%)** |
| − Collection & delivery (batched) | ~£6.00 |
| − Packaging | ~£1.50 |
| − Card processing (~2%) | ~£1.15 |
| **Net contribution** | **~£23.77 (≈41%)** |

The single biggest swing factor is **route density** — batching collections cuts the
per-order logistics cost. Concentrate Tier-1 postcodes before widening coverage.

---

## 8. Pricing guardrails

- Maintain **≥45% gross margin per item** after trade rate.
- The premium must be justified by **experience + aggregation**, never gouging.
- Re-price when trade rates, fuel, or competitor prices move materially.
- Keep specialist items (leather/bridal) priced for value — they're infrequent and
  high-trust; don't over-inflate.
